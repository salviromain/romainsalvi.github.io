About Me

I am a dual master's degree student in Computer Science and Data Science & Engineering at the University of Illinois Chicago and the Politecnico di Torino. I graduated with a 3.8 GPA at UIC and 110/110 at Politecnico di Torino, where I also completed my bachelor's degree in Information Engineering. My academic focus sits at the intersection of machine learning, statistics, big data systems, and large language models, with a strong emphasis on rigor and experimentation.

I am currently working as an AI Research Intern at Pirouette Software Inc., where I conduct research on the statistical properties of hardware anomaly traces generated by large language models. My work investigates whether emotionally driven computations differ measurably from neutral ones, combining low-level system monitoring via Linux performance counters with Python-based statistical analysis. Previously, I worked as a Data Science Intern at MEC srl, where I analyzed over 500,000 operational records to identify cost drivers, built automated ETL pipelines using Apache Airflow, and designed SQL-based dashboards to support real-time decision-making. In 2025, I also served as a Teaching Assistant at Politecnico di Torino for a Web Services course, mentoring more than 80 students on full-stack development, database design, and SQL-driven data access.

My main strengths lie in mathematics, statistics, and rigorous experimentation. I am particularly skilled at framing problems, engineering meaningful features, extracting signal from noisy data, and communicating insights clearly and effectively. I enjoy tackling complex problems from multiple perspectives and thrive in team environments where structure, discipline, and accountability matter.
